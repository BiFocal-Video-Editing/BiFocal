BiFocal: Target-Enhanced Video Editing
with Region-Specific Refinement

We propose BiFocal, a first-frame-based video editing method, which can perform target-directed and temporally consistent editing on real-world videos.

Abstract

As digital content creation and editing are in great demand, recent advances in diffusion models have paved the way for automated and high-quality video editing. However, most existing methods rely heavily on text prompts and thus suffer from challenges such as semantic ambiguity, attribute leakage, and insufficient local control. To address these issues, we propose BiFocal, a training-free image-guided video editing framework capable of precisely manipulating target regions while preserving unedited content. Specifically, BiFocal introduces a Selective Content Preservation and Editing (SCOPE) operation to explicitly optimize the reconstruction of non-edited regions, thereby enhancing background fidelity. In parallel, the proposed Target Enhanced Attention (TEA) module reinforces the texture consistency guided by reference image across edited frames, effectively mitigating temporal drift in target regions. Notably, our approach requires no extra fine-tuning or training. Extensive experiments demonstrate that BiFocal achieves competitive performance at aspect of visual quality, temporal consistency, and inference speed compared to other methods. These results highlight its potential as an effective and practical solution for high-quality video editing.

Method Overview

Top: We employ an off-the-shelf image editing model to modify the first frame using a reference image. Then during the process of synchronous denoising, selected features from the guidance branch are injected into the main generation branch to assist in editing the video. Bottom: We detail the two core components of our proposed method. The TEA module fuses the values of the edited first frame and subsequent frames using a mask derived from self-attention score maps. The SCOPE module leverages video segmentation masks to selectively retain background information tokens from the source video.

Ablation Study

We evaluate the impact of the Selective Content Preservation and Editing (SCOPE) module and the Target Enhanced Attention (TEA) module by entirely removing them from our pipeline. Without SCOPE, background fidelity significantly decreases, resulting in unwanted changes to non-edited regions. Meanwhile, the absence of TEA sometimes leads to increased temporal drift and reduced texture consistency in edited regions.
